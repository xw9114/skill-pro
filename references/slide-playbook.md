# Slide Playbook

## Page Taxonomy

Classify every slide before building it:

- Cover: set topic, tone, audience, occasion.
- Table of contents: show 3-5 sections only when navigation helps.
- Section divider: mark a meaningful turn in the argument.
- Content slide: make one claim and support it with one primary exhibit.
- Summary or close: synthesize implications, decisions, next steps, or Q&A.

## Content Archetypes

Use the archetype that matches the slide's job:

- Statement: one strong conclusion with minimal support.
- Evidence chart: one chart plus direct annotation of the takeaway.
- Comparison: before/after, options, vendors, scenarios, or tradeoffs.
- Matrix: two-axis positioning, prioritization, risk/impact, or capability map.
- Timeline: milestones, roadmap, historical development, rollout plan.
- Process: workflow, operating model, pipeline, architecture path.
- Framework: 3-5-part model, layered model, flywheel, stack, or capability blocks.
- Case example: situation, action, result, lesson.
- Data table: compact evidence where exact values matter.
- Screenshot or product view: real UI or artifact with callouts, not decoration.

## Layout Selection

- Dense analytical slide: use a strong title, left evidence, right implication panel, and compact source note.
- Executive update: use KPI row, one central chart, and clear variance callouts.
- Technical explanation: use labeled diagrams, grouped layers, and short step labels.
- Academic slide: use one figure, action title, citation, and minimal explanatory text.
- Training slide: use step-by-step sequence, example, and practice prompt.
- Pitch slide: use bold claim, proof point, and memorable visual contrast.

## Anti-Patterns

- Repeating title + bullets for most slides.
- Using icons as proof.
- Using decoration that does not encode meaning.
- Mixing unrelated evidence on one slide.
- Hiding the conclusion below the fold or in the speaker notes.
- Making every slide equally dense or equally sparse.
