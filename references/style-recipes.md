# Style Recipes

Use these only when the user has not provided a template or brand guide. Choose one recipe, then make it concrete with exact hex values, fonts, spacing, and type scale before creating slides.

## Executive Business

- Tone: quiet, decisive, boardroom-ready.
- Palette: off-white or charcoal base, restrained blue/green support, one sharp accent for decisions or risk.
- Type: Aptos Display or Arial for headings; Aptos or Arial for body.
- Layout: strong left alignment, compact KPI rows, clear chart annotations, conservative dividers.
- Avoid: decorative illustration, oversized hero treatment, icon grids.

## Technical Product

- Tone: precise, modern, systems-oriented.
- Palette: neutral canvas, dark ink, one product-coded accent, one status color.
- Type: Aptos, Arial, or IBM Plex Sans if available.
- Layout: architecture diagrams, process flows, annotated screenshots, comparison matrices.
- Avoid: vague stock imagery, unverified product UI, unlabeled arrows.

## Academic Analytical

- Tone: evidence-first, restrained, credible.
- Palette: white or near-white background, black text, one muted accent.
- Type: Aptos, Arial, or Georgia + Aptos when formality helps.
- Layout: one figure per slide, direct figure annotations, visible citation/source note.
- Avoid: decorative icons, clip art, exaggerated gradients, title accent lines.

## Investor or Pitch

- Tone: confident, memorable, commercially sharp.
- Palette: high-contrast base, one distinctive brand-like accent, sparse secondary colors.
- Type: bold display heading plus clean body font.
- Layout: big claim pages, traction charts, problem/solution contrast, customer proof, roadmap.
- Avoid: generic market-size bubbles without source, vanity metrics, overstuffed appendix slides.

## Training or Workshop

- Tone: approachable, structured, practical.
- Palette: warm neutral base, clear accent for steps, success/error colors for examples.
- Type: highly readable sans-serif.
- Layout: progression, examples, checklists, exercises, recap pages.
- Avoid: dense paragraphs, hidden instructions, tiny captions.

## Universal Sizing

- Cover title: 52-68 px.
- Section title: 44-56 px.
- Slide title: 30-40 px.
- Body text: 18-24 px.
- Chart/table labels: 14-16 px.
- Captions/source notes: 10-12 px.
- Keep line length short enough for projection; split slides before shrinking core text below readable sizes.
